Everything you need to know about the best Dinosaur

(Pterodactyls are actually not dinosaurs!)


Pterodactylus is a genus of pterosaurs, which were flying reptiles from the Mesozoic Era. While commonly referred to as 'pterodactyls,' it's important to clarify that 'pterodactyl' is not a specific scientific term for all pterosaurs. Instead, it's often used to describe various species of ancient flying reptiles. Pterosaurs constituted a diverse group of reptiles that independently evolved powered flight, distinct from birds and bats.


Pterosaurs were flying reptiles that lived during the time of the dinosaurs. They were around from 210 to 65 million years ago. They were the first vertebrates to evolve powered flight, and they were the largest animals to ever fly. Pterosaurs were not dinosaurs, but they were closely related to them. They were more closely related to crocodiles and birds than to dinosaurs.


There were many different species of pterosaurs. Some were as small as a sparrow, and some were as big as modern giraffes with a wingspan reaching 30 feet wide

228-66 million years ago

These ancient reptiles appeared to be capable of multiple modes of locomotion. Pterosaurs were skilled fliers with powerful flight muscles. It is estimated that they reached cruising speeds of roughly 90 kilometres per hour (km/h), even achieving speeds above 100 km/h for a few minutes. Some pterosaurs are also believed to have been able to swim, due to many of their fossils being found seaside, evidence of swim tracks, and some pterosaur diets consisting of aquatic prey. Despite their affinity for aquatic environments, research exploring pterosaur biomechanics suggests it is unlikely they were adept at simply floating on the surface of the water. They were also able to move about on land, with some species possibly even using all four limbs to walk, a hypothesis based on the fossilized terrestrial trackways of their footprints.
